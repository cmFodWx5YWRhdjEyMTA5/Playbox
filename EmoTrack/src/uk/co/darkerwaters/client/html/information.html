CBT Emotion Tracking

Tracking your emotions lies at the heart of CBT (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy. As part of treatment for all kinds of problems, including but not limited to; depression, anxiety, and chronic fatigue, it is a good idea to regularly track how you are feeling so when you look back on your week / month / year, you can properly see how you felt. Hopefully seeing that it was not as bad as you thought, and you are better over time. Personally, as I am suffering from Chronic Fatigue, I track:

  • Anxiety
  • Depression
  • Fatigue
Along with the built in:
  • Activity, I use a Misfit Shine fitness tracker watch to track a number of "Points" each day.
  • Sleep, the same fitness tracker I have records sleep and deep sleep amounts.
  • Events, mostly external triggers of Anxiety, Stress, and thence Fatigue.

Try to diligently track how you are feeling, the amount of sleep you are getting, and the activity you are managing. This is particularly useful and interesting if you are suffering, as I am, from Chronic Fatigue. Tracking sleep is a tricky one, I use a Misfit Shine fitness tracker I got from Amazon. There are lots of trackers available and more become available every week so I wont list or review them here. Just to say that I have found the Shine to be useful as its simple watch battery lasts over a month, it tracks activity while I cycle, and seems effective at tracking my sleep patterns. If you don't want to buy anything though, a simple step-tracker is only Â£10 or so, or just log the number of minutes you went walking for, and how long you were in bed. It is not the numbers that are important, just how they vary and change, so your guesses can be widely wrong, as long as they are consistently and relatively wrong, the tracking still works.

When you have tracked some data, check out the graphs and the "Analysis" tab. It should show you how your negative emotions are coming down over time, and how external events affect your mood. In the case of fatigue, it should be that a consistent level of activity helps, but too much or too little causes a blip in your fatigue. Personally I found this to be true, however, the largest contributing factor to mine were external work stresses. Something that helped me speed my recovery significantly by dealing with, and reducing, these external sources of Anxiety.
